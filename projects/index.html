Expanding a Sum to Show a Wronskian Identity

Feb 2026 - Ongoing

We notice that the equation$$\sum_{\sigma \in S_N} (-1)^\sigma \, w_{\sigma(1)}(x) \cdots w_{\sigma(N)}^{(p)}(x) = \text{const}(p) \cdot \text{Wronskian}(w_1, \dots, w_N) \cdot f^{(p)}(x)$$ holds for $p=1,2$. Thus, we conjecture that for all $p \in \mathbb{N}$, the equation holds, and we try to calculate $\text{const}(p)$ for each $p$.

Construction of Two Infinite Families of Minimal Surfaces

Sep 2025 - Ongoing

The solutions of the minimal surface equation, $$\left(1+u_y^2\right)u_{xx}-2u_xu_yu_{xy}+\left(1+u_x^2\right)u_{yy}=0,$$ describe two-dimensional minimal surfaces $\Sigma\subset\mathbb{E}^3$ in nonparametric form.

In his 2006 paper, Dr. Kiselev provides us with two infinite families of minimal surfaces. I construct further examples from these families using a combination of differential geometry techniques. The first surface from one of the families is a self-intersecting minimal surface filling the trefoil knot, and I believe exploring these surfaces further would contribute to Algebraic Topology in an unexpected way.
