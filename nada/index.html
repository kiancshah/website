Dearest Nada,

Naturally, life has not been the same since we parted. In my futile attempts to get rid of my emptiness, my hollowness, I only end up carving deeper into the tiniest bits of pain remaining. You left, and the colours left with you - my love for cooking, my humanitarian side, my celebrations after my achievements - acts of self-preservation at best.

However, as promised, I don't remember us by the tears we shed but rather the cute, perfect, precious moments we had. And in one of these moments, you asked me to be your valentine, and I not only said yes but asked for you to wait for me. Thus, take this as simply a promise fulfilled. I have planned to make one of these valentines website for you for months now. And that's all it is. We shared a more real connection than I have with anyone in my life, and I believe this deserves to be celebrated on valentines day.

I'll stop now. I don't want to pour my heart out for you to believe I'm being manipulative and what not. You can choose to believe whichever words you want, for almost none of the words after this essay are mine. Click on any of the photos to see what I felt - these are all excerpts from movies, books, and songs, which I believe is the truest form of communication.

You and I both know that I have always loved you. I'm sorry for never finding the perfect moment to say it.

Stay cute and true to your heart.

With all my love,
Kian
